🌈 Welcome to the World of Sounds!

Before children read words, they need to notice that spoken words are made of sound parts.

In this preview, you will try a few activities the way a young learner might experience them.

Words can also be broken into OTHER sound parts too.

Level 2

🕵️ Sound Detective Mission

Phonemic awareness is about spoken sounds. It does not require written letters.

1. Click the activities that help children focus on sounds.

Level 3

🧩 Big Parts, Small Parts

🐘 elephant
Words can be broken into larger parts first. Syllables are usually easier to hear than tiny sounds.

1. Clap the syllables in elephant. How many syllables do you hear?

Level 4

🎈 Phoneme Counter

Phonemes are the smallest individual sounds we hear in spoken words.

1. How many phonemes do you hear in ship?

Level 5

🎈 Another Phoneme Counter

Some words have spellings that look long, but the number of sounds may be different.

1. How many phonemes do you hear in frog?

Level 6

👏 Syllable Detective

🦒 giraffe
Syllables are larger sound chunks that children can often hear by clapping.

1. How many syllables do you hear in giraffe?

Level 7

✏️ Grapheme Match

A grapheme is the written spelling that represents a sound.

1. Which graphemes can represent the sound /f/?
